## Your digital toolkit for ultimate discretion

Observe, the privacy triforce:

Booking an escort is just like finding and contracting the services of any other professional. Very simply, the process looks like this:

1. Find the professional you want to book.
1. Get in touch with that professional and request a quote for the service you want.
1. Pay a deposit and ultimately your total amount due.

In the physical world, you find a professional to book by walking around on the street. This is the analog equivalent of Web browsing. You will see storefronts with big signs like "Joe's Famous Pizza," "Keys Cut In One Hour," or "Best Dry Cleaning" that tell you what the service on offer is.

Eventually, you need to talk to someone or otherwise engage with the business to learn more and ultimately confirm an appointment. This might mean talking to a receptionist, clerk, or store associate. When you're not physically in a store, it means you have to use a messaging application or perhaps a Web form to make your initial contact.

Finally, if you decide to proceed with this specific business, you have to pay them. Paying cash is only possible if you're physically present, such as if you're standing next to a cashier in person. You could of course also insert, swipe, or tap your payment card, but the benefit of electronic money is that you can also pay when you are at a far away place. Either way, confirming payment is how you seal the deal.

**All of these steps have encrypted, private, digital counterparts.**

All you have to do to ensure that the Internet has absolutely no trace of your interaction with such a business is use the tool that protects that part of the above process.

### Tor Browser: Safe, anonymous Web browsing

Escorts like me typically [advertise on specialty Web sites]({% link index.html %}#links-group-ad-listings), called escort malls. The analogy is obvious! Browsing an escort site is the online equivalent of walking around in a real-world mall where each of us have a store front (our ad listing).

You probably know that when you browse the Internet with a normal Web browser, most of your browsing activity is being tracked. Even if you switch your browser into a "private browsing" or "incognito" mode, your Internet service provider (your ISP, like Verizon, Comcast, Rogers, Telstra, iiNet, or whoever) knows who you are and what sites you're visiting.

**The safest way to browse the Web anonymously is to use [Tor Browser](https://torproject.org/){:target="_blank"}**, a specialty Web browser made for one purpose and one purpose only: shielding everything you do on the Web from prying eyes. Tor Browser is free, easy to use, and available on desktop, Android, and Apple iOS devices.

{:.align-center}
{% include link-as-button.md anchor_text="Get Tor Browser" url="https://torproject.org/" target="_blank" %}

Basically, Tor Browser gives everyone who uses it the same non-descript disguise so, as long as you don't voluntarily divulge personal details to a Web site you visit, there's no way for anyone to know that you (as opposed to any other similarly-disguised, non-descript Tor Browser user) are the one walking around the mall.

What this means when you browse a website with Tor Browser:

* Your <abbr title="Internet Service Provider">ISP</abbr> only knows you are using Tor, not which site you're actually browsing.
    * For extreme levels of privacy, you can [use a Tor bridge](https://support.torproject.org/tor-browser/circumvention/using-bridges/) to hide the fact that you are even using Tor in the first place.
* The website you're visiting knows only that a Tor Browser user is visiting their site, not which individual user you are.
    * For extreme levels of protection from the website itself, [adjust Tor Browser's security level](https://support.torproject.org/tor-browser/features/security-levels/) to `Safest`.

### Signal Private Messenger: Actually private messaging

When booking an escort, you must reach out to them with a polite, succinct, informative message. This is no different than making an appointment with a hairdresser, plumber, tailor, or other professional. You need to identify and introduce yourself (we call this "[screening]({% link _faq/screening/why-screen.md %})"), and request a specific service at a specific date and time (we call this "[inquiring]({% link resources/for-clients/booking-guide/index.md %})").

[Privacy-conscious escorts like me make use of encrypted communications channels]({% link resources/for-providers/signal-for-sex-work/index.md %}) so that when you send us a message like this, [you can be confident that it is for our eyes only]({% link colophon/index.md %}#privacy-guarantees-of-first-contact). **The best encrypted messaging platform is [Signal Private Messenger]({% link resources/for-clients/messaging-discreetly/index.md %}#ultimate-privacy-signal-private-messenger-with-screen-lock)** because it is free, globally available, solidly reliable, wildly performant, widely adopted, and has best-in-class encryption and metadata protection features.

{:.align-center}

What this means when you use Signal to contact an escort:

* Your <abbr title="Internet Service Provider">ISP</abbr> only knows that you are using Signal Private Messenger, not whom you are speaking to.
    * You can even combine Signal with Tor so that your ISP doesn't even know you're using Signal.
* If the escort you're contacting has enabled [certain "sealed sender" sender-privacy features]({% link resources/for-providers/signal-for-sex-work/index.md %}#allow-sealed-sender-from-anyone), as I have, Signal does not know that *you* are contacting them, just that someone did.
* The escort you're contacting knows only what you share in your Signal Profile and in your messages to them about you.
    * Using Signal does not exempt you from their [screening process]({% link _faq/screening/screening-process.md %}).
    * Just like any other businessperson, they can and will refuse to service you and may even block you on the app if you refuse to adhere to their safety, etiquette, and hygiene protocols.
* Only the escort you contact can read the contents of your messages. No third party, including Signal itself, can read the content of your messages.
    * This is what ensures that your screening information also remains strictly confidential.

### Shielded Zcash: Encrypted electronic money

The final step in the booking process is making a small advance payment called a [deposit]({% link _faq/booking/what-is-a-deposit.md %}).

* Since this payment is required *before* you meet your worker, [paper money is not a suitable payment method for a deposit]({% link _faq/booking/payment-methods.md %}).
* If you don't want to be seen paying an escort, however, traditional electronic payment methods also won't do.
    * When you make a purchase with a payment card, the payment processor (Visa, MasterCard, etcetera) learns of your purchase.
    * The same is true of using payment apps, such as CashApp, PayPal, or Venmo.
    * Bank transfers such as Zelle or international payment services like Wise are no better, and sometimes much worse.

This is where [using cryptocurrency]({% link resources/for-clients/using-cryptocurrency/index.md %}) comes in. Especially savvy escorts like me can [privately accept any number of cryptocurrencies]({% link resources/for-providers/using-cryptocurrency/on-ramping/index.md %}#getting-paid-in-zcash-when-your-client-or-customer-only-has-bitcoin), but if you pay using privacy-preserving cryptocurrencies like [shielded Zcash (ZEC)](https://z.cash/learn/what-is-the-difference-between-shielded-and-transparent-zcash/){:target="_blank"}, you protect your privacy even more.

**The best shielded Zcash cryptocurrency wallet app is Zodl** because it makes using private cryptocurrency absurdly easy and protects you from making simple mistakes such as making payments from unshielded (public) addresses.

{:.align-center}
{% include link-as-button.md anchor_text="Get Zodl Wallet" url="https://zodl.com/" target="_blank" %}

When you use shielded Zcash to make a payment:

* No one who isn't a party to the transaction can see the transaction details.
    * The recipient (your worker) sees only the amount they receive and that they are the recipient. They cannot see the sender's wallet address.
    * The sender (you) can see the recipient's wallet address and the amount you sent.
* Your <abbr title="Internet Service Provider">ISP</abbr> knows you made a Zcash payment, but does not learn anything about your payment.
    * For extreme levels of privacy, you can enable Tor from within the Zodl app, which hides your use of the Zcash network from your ISP.
* Zcash network operators know that someone at your IP address is using the network, but they cannot learn the details of your transactions.
    * When you enable Tor within Zodl, the Zcash network operators know only that a Tor user accessed the network, not that you are the given user making a payment.
